Jumbotron

Sometimes useful at the top of pages (for announcements, etc) but not mandatory.

All the usual formatting can be added within this Jumbotron box, but it will always appear larger than normal. The H1 style at the top may be too large for most purposes, so avoid it.

This is a "shortcodes" page.

This should not be thought of as a standard content page, but as a page where all of the available formatting appears in one place. Obviously most pages shouldn't look this busy.

"Shortcodes" is the normal name used for a page which defines all of the styles available. These styles may be used on other pages in a variety of ways. Depending on the specific requirements and functions of a page (or part of a page) you will be able to apply these styles to content. Some restrictions will apply, mainly due to the complexity of some layouts, the size of the content spaces, or the way we automatically generate some parts of the page for you.

This page is not intended to show how the page layout looks (i.e. the specific location of type and images). It is just to define how each item of type looks, the sizes of images, colours and styles for widgets, tables and embedded code.

Look at the other pages in this section for details of how these styles are applied to page templates.

Images which resize to fit

On a responsive website, there is no longer a concept of a fixed-size element. All images and videos must scale to fit the screen on which they appear.

For this reason, images are produced at 25%, 33%, 50% or 100% of the width of content space. Examples are shown below:

On some screens it will wrap, on others it won't, because the larger the screen, the wider the space, and therefore the fewer lines text requires. The width of the image and the amount of text determing how the page looks. Note that the size (25%, 33%, 50%) only happen if the screen is large enough. Embedded YouTube or Vimeo videos

Videos are always 100% the width of the content space. Videos come in 2 aspect ratios (16:9 and 4:3). To make the video behave properly you must know the aspect ratio, which is usually displayed on the YouTube page where the vido appears. Below is the same video in both aspect rations, so you can see the difference.

The video below is 4:3, and is less "widescreen" and taller. Because the aspect ratio is incorrect for this video, it will appear warped.

You can use the CMS to embed Google maps, which will be displayed as shown here.

Google maps adds additional functionality not available if you simply create the image of a map. However, we have little control over how the map looks. There are lots of free services which allow you to customise what appears on Google maps before you embed it into the site. Google's own tool is good, and there are full instructions and help files provided by Google.

Note we do not offer free training and support for Google maps, because all of that information is already provided by Google.
